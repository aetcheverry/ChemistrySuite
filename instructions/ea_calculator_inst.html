Elemental Analysis

Composition Analysis & Solvent Fitting

The Elemental Analysis (EA) module allows you to calculate theoretical percentages for Carbon, Hydrogen, Nitrogen, and other elements. Crucially, it includes a "Best Fit" solver that helps identify occluded solvents or impurities by matching experimental data to theoretical compositions.

1. Theoretical Calculation
Calculating % Composition

To obtain the theoretical elemental percentages for a pure compound:

Steps:
  1. Enter the Chemical Formula of your compound (e.g., C10H22O or C6H5Br).
  2. Leave the Experimental % fields as 0.00.
  3. Click Calculate Best Fit.

The results box will display the theoretical mass percentages for all elements found in the formula.

2. Solvent Fitting (Best Fit)
Matching Experimental Data

If your experimental EA results do not match the theoretical values, your sample may contain trapped solvents or inorganic salts. This tool iterates through combinations of solvents to minimize the error (Delta).

Steps:
  1. Enter your Chemical Formula.
  2. Enter the values obtained from the lab in the Experimental % fields (C, H, N, etc.).
  3. Select Solvent Candidates:
    • Choose likely solvents (e.g., Water, DCM, Ethyl Acetate) from the dropdowns.
    • Set the Min and Max equivalents (e.g., 0 to 2.0).
  4. Adjust the Increment (step size) for higher precision (default is 0.1 eq).
  5. Click Calculate Best Fit.
The Algorithm: The calculator tests every combination of solvent equivalents (within your defined range and increment) and returns the composition that results in the smallest total deviation from your experimental numbers.
3. Interpreting Results
Understanding the Output
  • Composition: The final proposed formula including solvates (e.g., Compound · 0.5 H2O).
  • Theoretical: The calculated percentages for the proposed composition.
  • Found: Your input experimental values.
  • Delta (Δ): The absolute difference between Theoretical and Found. Ideally, this should be < 0.4% for publication quality.
Utility Features
Reporting & Export
  • Copy Results (Ctrl+C): Copies the summary table and composition to the clipboard.
  • Copy Report (Ctrl+Shift+C): Copies a full text report, including the calculation parameters and detailed error analysis, suitable for archiving.
