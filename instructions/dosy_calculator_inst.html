DOSY NMR Calculator

Diffusion Coefficient & Molecular Weight Estimation

This module assists in interpreting Diffusion Ordered Spectroscopy (DOSY) data. It allows you to interconvert between Diffusion Coefficients (D), Hydrodynamic Radii (rH), and estimated Molecular Weights (MW) using two different models.

1. Stokes-Einstein Equation
Formula: D = kBT / (c π η rH)

The classical model for a spherical particle moving through a continuum fluid. Use this to determine the physical size (rH) of your molecule.

Steps:
  1. Select your Solvent. The Viscosity (η) is automatically set for 298.15 K.
  2. Adjust the Temperature if your experiment was not at 25°C.
  3. Shape Factor Slider: Adjust the friction coefficient (c):
    • 6.00 π (Stick): Standard for larger molecules; assumes solvent sticks to the surface.
    • 4.00 π (Slip): Often better for small molecules where solvent flows past the surface.
  4. Calculation:
    • Input rH: Enter your experimental D or logD to estimate the hydrodynamic radius.
    • Input D: Enter a hypothetical hydrodynamic radius rH to estimate D.
2. SEGWE Method
SEGWE Method

SEGWE stands for Stokes-Einstein Gierer-Wirtz Estimation. This uses empirical correlations tailored for small molecules in specific NMR solvents.

Steps:
  1. Select the Solvent (Method relies on specific solvent parameters).
  2. Ensure the Temperature matches your experiment.
  3. Calculation:
    • Input D: Enter your diffusion coefficient (D or logD) to estimate MW and rH.
    • Input MW: Enter a target MW to predict the expected D and rH.
    • Input rH: Enter a hypothetical hydrodynamic radius rH to estimate D and MW.
This method is often more accurate for small organic molecules/pharmaceuticals than the pure Stokes-Einstein spherical approximation.
Utility Features
Reporting
