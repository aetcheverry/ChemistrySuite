Dilution Calculator

Instructions & Usage Guide

The Dilution Calculator module provides three distinct tools to assist with daily laboratory preparations. Select the appropriate section based on whether you are diluting a stock solution, preparing acid/base from a liquid reagent, or dissolving a solid.

1. General Solution Dilution
Formula: C1V1 = C2V2

Use this tool when you have a stock solution of known concentration and wish to prepare a specific volume of a more dilute solution.

Steps:
  1. Enter the Stock Concentration (C1).
  2. Enter the desired Final Volume (V2).
  3. Enter the desired Final Concentration (C2).
  4. Click Calculate.

The result will display the volume of stock solution (V1) required. The remaining volume should be made up with your solvent.

2. Acids & Bases (Liquid Stocks)
Preparation from Commercial Reagents

Commercial acids and bases are often sold as weight percentages (e.g., 37% HCl) with specific densities. This tool calculates the molarity of the commercial bottle and determines how much to add to reach your target concentration.

Steps:
  1. Select a reagent from the Reagent dropdown (e.g., Hydrochloric Acid, Sulfuric Acid).
  2. Set the Target Volume and Target Molarity you wish to prepare.
  3. Click Calculate.
Custom Reagents: If your reagent is not listed, select "Other (Manual Input)" from the dropdown. Additional fields will appear allowing you to manually enter the Density (g/mL), Purity (%), and Molecular Weight (MW) found on the bottle label.
3. Mass Calculation (Solid to Liquid)
Preparing Solutions from Solids

Calculate the mass of a solid compound required to achieve a specific molarity in a given volume.

Steps:
  1. Chemical Formula: Enter the formula (e.g., NaCl, C6H12O6). The Molecular Weight (MW) will be calculated automatically.
  2. Manual MW: Alternatively, if the formula is complex or unknown, you can manually type the MW into the number box.
  3. Enter the Target Volume and Target Concentration.
  4. Click Calculate.
Utility Features
Shortcuts & Export
  • Clear Inputs (Ctrl+X): Resets all fields in the currently active calculator to their default values.
  • Copy Results (Ctrl+C): Copies the text from the result boxes to your clipboard for use in lab notebooks.
